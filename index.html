Canada builds world-class companies. It struggles to keep them.

Canada produces a tenth of the world's top AI researchers, a deep university base, and a steady supply of globally competitive companies. Yet its scale-up capital is overwhelmingly foreign, its champions are acquired or list abroad, and its public markets have thinned. This report traces the problem from the exchanges to the companies — and sets out what to do about it.

About this report

A synthesis of a multi-month capital-markets research program held in an Obsidian research vault — public-markets structure, the financing gap, ~34 company case studies, international comparators, and a costed policy toolkit. Every headline figure traces to a single validated data layer; the Methodology page grades each by confidence (government/regulatory = high; industry/CVCA/NACO = medium; advocacy/aggregator and private valuations = soft) and states its caveats.

A research synthesis and proof-of-concept, not a published government report. Toggle light / dark from the top bar.

Sources & confidence: every figure carries a source pill — tap it for the figure's provenance and confidence grade · full register on the Methodology page.
