Canada

◆ Subject

A large, prosperous economy that builds world-class capability but captures too little of the return — big on aggregate market size, thin on R&D intensity and venture capital, and losing founders and champions abroad.

Canada carries two live VC/GDP bases, never mixed: 0.35% mid-pack on the Compendium §3.1 series (charted on the Comparators page), and 0.20% on the OECD/Landscape basis that the dollar-gap arithmetic depends on. This is the subject at a glance — the full treatment lives in the report chapters below.

Public markets
Capital Pool Company (CPC) program 1986
TSXV listing vehicle: principals IPO a cash shell that then completes a Qualifying Transaction with a private operating business (seed + IPO typically C$200K–C$5M).
Outcome 2,850+ CPCs listed since 1986 at ~86% QT completion; 27 QTs closed in 2025; ~80 former CPCs now trade on the TSX.
Private capital
Venture Capital Action Plan (VCAP) 2013
$390M federal allocation into fund-of-funds, leveraging private and provincial capital.
Outcome $1.4B total raised at 3.7:1 leverage; now fully committed.
Venture Capital Catalyst Initiative (VCCI) 2017 (2021, 2026 tranches)
Successor fund-of-funds mobilising private and institutional VC. VCCI 2017 $372M; VCCI 2021 $450M late-stage; VCCI 4.0 (2026) $1B over three years via BDC.
Outcome VCCI 2017 → $1.9B total at 5.1:1 leverage; VCCI 2021 $448.5M committed as at Mar 31 2025.
Business Development Bank of Canada (BDC)
Federal development bank: SME loans (to $5M), the VCCI fund-of-funds, a $4B defence/security platform (2025), and a Deep Tech Venture Fund.
Outcome Capital Incentive Programs portfolio $1.63B at fair value (BDC Annual Report 2025).
Flow-Through Shares (+ METC / CMETC) 1972 (METC 2000, CMETC 2022)
Lets resource companies flow 100% of Canadian Exploration Expenses to investors, layered with a 15% METC / 30% critical-mineral CMETC. A uniquely Canadian resource-financing stack, not innovation-targeted.
Outcome Since 2011, 60% of mines opened in Ontario were explored by juniors using FTS ($4.3B mine-construction capital); Jog (2016): investors lost $1.2B on $2.55B invested 2008–2012.
Labour-Sponsored Venture Capital Corporations (LSVCCs) 1988 (federal credit)
15% federal tax credit (with provincial + RRSP stacking) to channel retail savings into VC; federal credit phased out by 2017, Ontario's 2010–2012. Quebec's Fonds de solidarité FTQ (~C$23B AUM) remains.
Outcome Cautionary: reduced the aggregate Canadian VC pool by ~C$1B/year (~400 investments); industry returns ~−3% 1995–2005.
Growth companies
Invest Ontario
Ontario investment-attraction agency; modular/prefab housing support at C$50M over 5 years (Element5, Sekisui, Assembly Corp.).
Outcome none stated.
Scaling & retention
National AI Strategy 'AI for All' (incl. Canadian Tech Growth Fund) 2026
Launched June 4 2026: ~C$2.3B identified spending; the scale-up pillar is a C$500M Canadian Tech Growth Fund that takes equity stakes to help firms attract private capital, retain talent/IP, and stay anchored in Canada.
Outcome none stated (program new).
Innovation & R&D
Scientific Research & Experimental Development (SR&ED) tax credit n/a (Budget 2025 enhancements)
CRA-administered R&D tax credit: 35% refundable for CCPCs (expenditure limit raised to C$6M), 15% non-refundable otherwise; Ontario layers OITC/OBRITC on top.
Outcome 2024–25: 22,758 claims filed; C$4.7B claimed, C$4.5B allowed; 90% accepted as filed.

Public markets
PISCES (Private Intermittent Securities and Capital Exchange System) 2024 legislation; 2025 launch
The world's first regulated secondary market for private-company shares — intermittent trading windows, secondary-only, lighter disclosure than AIM.
Outcome LSE's Private Securities Market approved as first operator (Aug 26 2025); first auction 25 March 2026 (Oxford Science Enterprises, £1.3B).
Private capital
EIS (Enterprise Investment Scheme) 1994
30% income-tax relief + CGT exemption + loss relief on qualifying early-stage investments.
Outcome ~£30B channelled into 53,000+ companies since inception; 46.5% of UK unicorns received EIS investment.
SEIS (Seed Enterprise Investment Scheme) 2012 (expanded 2023)
50% income-tax relief on seed investments up to £200,000/yr; full CGT exemption after three years.
Outcome 2023–24 SEIS funding rose 51% YoY to £242M; companies funded 1,835 → 2,290.
Mansion House Compact / Accord 2023 (Accord May 2025)
Voluntary DC-pension pledge, backed by a statutory reserve power to mandate; the 2025 Accord raised the commitment to 10% private markets with ≥5% in UK assets by 2030 across 17 providers (~£252B in scope).
Outcome Underdelivered — UK DC schemes still direct ~3.5% to private markets (0.5% PE/VC); ~0.6% unlisted-equity achieved vs a 5% goal.
LTAF / Productive Finance Roadmap 2021
The BoE/HMT/FCA roadmap and the Long-Term Asset Fund vehicle designed to make illiquid assets tractable for DC pension schemes; ISA-eligible from April 2026.
Outcome none stated (Aviva Investors now manages four LTAFs).
Innovation & R&D
FCA Regulatory Sandbox 2016
The world's first regulatory sandbox — authorised firms test innovative products with real customers under FCA supervision.
Outcome 118 of 375 applicants selected; graduates raised 15% higher valuations and 6.6× larger amounts post-graduation.

Sweden

Comparator

Coherent, mutually-reinforcing policy — a 1990s tax reset, the ISK retail wrapper, employee-option reform and a deep growth board — that produced Europe's densest unicorn output and a self-reinforcing founder flywheel.

Public markets
ISK (Investeringssparkonto) investment savings account 2012
A retail flat-tax investment wrapper (~0.9%/yr on account value, no CGT reporting) that built a deep domestic equity base.
Outcome 3.8 million Swedes (of ~10M) hold an ISK, ~SEK 1.665 trillion (~C$220B). The often-cited 35%-of-trading figure in the 10 days after Volvo Car's IPO is an illustrative one-event snapshot of retail participation, not a demonstrated ISK effect.
Growth companies
Nasdaq First North (growth market)
A light-touch MTF that acts as a graduation stepping-stone to the regulated main market.
Outcome 551 companies (61% of domestic listings) across Sweden's three MTFs — the EU's largest MTF segment; First North companies grew revenue 200% faster and created 2× the jobs of unlisted peers (Nasdaq study).
Scaling & retention
QESO (Qualified Employee Stock Options) 2018 (expanded 2022)
Options vest and exercise without triggering salary income or employer social charges; the full gain is taxed only as capital income on disposal (~30% vs ~55%+).
Outcome none stated (revenue/asset threshold raised to SEK 280M in 2022).
Founder flywheel + entrepreneurship leave
Favourable option/CGT treatment plus a right to six months' unpaid entrepreneurship leave recycles founder wealth into the next generation.
Outcome Spotify alumni: 124+ VC-backed startups; Klarna alumni: 91+; King alumni: 39+.
Innovation & R&D
The 1990–91 Tax Reform 1990–91
Cut corporate tax from 52% to 30% (later 20.6%), introduced a flat 30% capital-income tax and neutrality — the base layer of the model.
Outcome Per-capita GDP growth rose to 3.4%/yr (1994–2007) vs 2.4% in the eurozone; patent activity per resident surged post-1991.

Comparator

Private capital
Yozma 1993
Government-owned VC company capitalised at US$100M: 40% public equity in ten new private funds plus a cheap 5-year buyout option; each fund required a foreign VC partner and 60%+ private capital.
Outcome Israeli annual VC deployment grew ~60-fold — from ~US$58M (1991) to ~US$3.3B (2000); ~164 portfolio companies; Yozma fully privatised by 1998.
Yozma Fund 2.0 2024
Institutional matching (US$0.30 of government capital per US$1.00 of institutional LP commitment) plus a deep-tech direct track.
Outcome ~US$450M Phase 1 deployment; massively oversubscribed (demand exceeded IIA capacity by ~US$474M); projected to channel ~US$2B.
Scaling & retention
Unit 8200 / Talpiot military-to-startup pipeline structural feature
Elite IDF intelligence and technology units function as de facto incubators, feeding a dense founder network that keeps R&D in Israel. A cultural/institutional feature, not a transferable policy instrument — the vault flags it as not directly replicable.
Context Over 20% of Israeli startup founders served in elite IDF units; by Aug 2024, five US-listed Unit 8200-founded companies had ~US$160B combined market cap.
Innovation & R&D
BIRD Foundation 1977
Binational Israel-US R&D fund pairing a US and Israeli company, contributing up to 50% of project costs.
Outcome By 1992, ~60% of Israeli companies on the NYSE and 75% on NASDAQ had been BIRD-supported.
R&D Law / Office of the Chief Scientist (OCS) 1984
Conditional grants of 20–50% of approved R&D budgets, repayable through royalties only on commercial success, with no state equity; IP kept in Israel absent approval.
Outcome none stated.

A single 30% flat rate on dividends, interest and capital gains, replacing a marginal system that could exceed 62%.
Outcome none stated.
Growth companies
La French Tech (Next40/FT120 + French Tech Visa) 2014 (visa 2017)
Government branding/coordination plus an annual selection of the most promising scale-ups and a 4-year founder visa, aimed at retention and scaling.
Outcome 2025 Next40: one-third generate >€100M revenue, 42,000 direct jobs, 93% with international presence, eight unicorns.
Loi PACTE 2019
Single-statute business-law reform bundling simplified incorporation, employee equity, bankruptcy reform and the PER retirement-savings product (which can invest in unlisted equity/VC).
Outcome By end-2024, 11.6M PER account holders held ~€125.7B (~C$188B).
Scaling & retention
BSPCE (founder/employee share warrants) 2019 (PACTE reform)
France's principal startup employee stock-option vehicle; PACTE broadened eligibility and gains benefit from the flat 30% PFU.
Outcome none stated.
