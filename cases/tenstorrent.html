01 The capital journey

Foreign strategic capital, and a domicile that followed it.

Chip design is brutally capital-intensive, and Tenstorrent's nine-figure rounds have been led by US financial investors and Korean industrial conglomerates. Canadian institutions appear — but only as followers.

02 The defining moment
The quiet redomicile — HQ to Santa Clara, Delaware holdco.
In late 2023 Tenstorrent moved its headquarters to Santa Clara and re-incorporated under a Delaware holding company (Tenstorrent Holdings). The Logic reported the step was investor-driven — a large investor faced an ownership cap if the company stayed domiciled outside the US, and a US base was framed as a common step ahead of a Nasdaq/NYSE IPO. Tenstorrent kept a Toronto office (~140 staff at the 2024 round) and still calls Canada a development hub.
Why it mattered: this is the textbook Flight-to-the-US mechanism — foreign capital with US-IPO ambitions pulling domicile out of Canada even as the engineering stays. Unlike Shopify, Tenstorrent has no founder-control or governance moat keeping it Canadian.

03 The policy lens

What would have changed the outcome.

The retention levers that bind here are largely federal industrial policy and the domestic capacity to lead deep-tech rounds — areas where securities policy is only indirect.

No governance moat the Shopify gap
No founder-control or anti-takeover structure keeps it Canadian — the cap table actively pulls toward the US and Asia. Lever ③ Retention.
Domestic capital only follows never leads
HOOPP and EDC joined the Series D — but as followers in a foreign-led round. Mobilizing patient/institutional capital to lead nine-figure deep-tech rounds is Lever ① Supply.
Foundry / node dependence beyond securities policy
Fabless and dependent on TSMC/Samsung; Canada has no advanced-node fab. A federal semiconductor industrial strategy, not an OSC lever.
The domicile-cap mechanic a deal-design question
An investor's ownership cap drove the US re-domiciliation. Listing/retention incentives and deal structures that preserve Canadian domicile are the concrete lever.
LEVER ① + ③ · SUPPLY & RETENTION
Lead the round, then keep the domicile
Domestic capital that anchors deep-tech rounds instead of following, plus retention incentives that make staying domiciled the rational choice.
