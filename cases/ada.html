02 The defining moment
The growth-stage handoff.
Ada is a clean picture of the vault's scale hypothesis. It was seeded with Canadian participation — Version One at the seed, Leaders Fund at the Series A — but when it reached the value-defining rounds, every growth-stage dollar came from US funds: Accel at the Series B, then Spark Capital and Tiger Global leading the US$130M Series C that minted the US$1.2B unicorn. Canadian angels and seed capital exist; Canadian growth equity does not, so a scaling company imports US capital — and, with it, US influence over the timing and venue of an exit.
Why it mattered: Ada has not relocated — HQ, founders and brand are still in Toronto, distinguishing it from a completed loss like Element AI. But a US-led cap table, a US-weighted enterprise customer base (Verizon, Square, Meta, Pinterest) and US strategic acquirers across the agentic-customer-service category together create the standing conditions for a US exit. Ada is the live version of the flight dynamic, not a finished one.

03 The policy lens

What would keep the applied-AI layer Canadian.

Ada is retained for now, so the lens is which levers would deepen the anchor before the exit conditions bind — and where the vulnerabilities already sit.

Growth equity is entirely foreign the core gap — supply-side
Domestic funds led the seed and A; every dollar from the Series B up was US. Fund-of-funds + matching and institutional mobilization are the direct fix — Lever ① Supply (H-S1 / H-S2), with an investor tax incentive to crowd in (H-C1).
Applied AI is where AI monetizes replicable — demand-side
Ada is a deployed, revenue-generating AI product, not AI branding. Government-as-customer procurement of domestic applied AI deepens commercial embeddedness — a demand-side lever.
Exit path routes offshore the standing risk
The most plausible exits — acquisition by a US CX/CRM incumbent, or a US listing — send value and control south. Retention conditionality and anti-take-private tools sit at Lever ③ Retention; a credible TSX venue at Lever ④ Liquidity.
Stale mark, no domestic anchor the open vulnerability
No priced round since 2021 and no domestic public-market holder mean nothing embeds Canadian institutions in the cap table. Category margin pressure (Sierra, Decagon, Intercom, Salesforce) sharpens the timing.
LEVER ① · SUPPLY (+ DEMAND)
Domestic growth capital at Series B / C
Fund-of-funds and institutional mobilization that can lead the growth rounds US funds now fill, plus procurement of domestic applied AI — the two levers Ada's cap table points straight at.
04 Replicability verdict

Retained for now — but flight-exposed by design of the cap table.

Retained · flight-exposed
Seeded in Canada, scaled on American money.
Ada kept its HQ, founders and brand in Toronto and has not relocated — unlike Element AI, the applied-AI champion that was lost to ServiceNow. It fills the layer where AI actually monetizes, alongside Cohere (frontier) and Waabi (autonomy). But its growth equity is entirely US-led, its enterprise base is US-weighted, and the plausible exits — a US CX/CRM acquirer or a US listing — route control offshore. The replicable lever is domestic growth capital at the Series B/C stage; the risk is that with a stale 2021 mark and no domestic anchor, the standing conditions all point south.
Ada was seeded in Canada and scaled on American money. Where it lists — or sells — will decide whether it stays.
