03 Key caveats

Where to be careful.

The honest reading of any synthesis is in its caveats. These are the limits a careful reader should hold in mind.

Market caps are point-in-time
TSX/TSXV as at May 2026; CSE end-2025. Several names have moved materially since the snapshot — treat them as a dated cross-section, not live quotes.
Private valuations are last-priced ⚠️
Figures for private champions are last-priced or press-reported rounds, flagged with ⚠️. They are not marks, audited values, or current.
Investor-domicile is indicative
"US-backed" / "Canadian-backed" tags reflect lead or notable investors, not full cap-table control or board math.
"AI company" is curated
The AI cohort is a curated list, not a bottom-up definition. Different definitions would shift counts at the margin.
The financing gap is a computation
Canada's VC/GDP vs. the comparator median and the US benchmark — a modelled estimate with a stated range, not an observed figure.
Two VC/GDP figures, two bases
The report cites Canada's venture capital at 0.20% of GDP on the diagnosis pages (OECD Entrepreneurship Financing DB, 2024 — the Ontario Financing Gap Landscape basis) and at ~0.35% on the Comparators page and the second, 15-economy chart on Financing Gap §01b (Compendium §3.1 — a CVCA / KPMG / NVCA / OECD blend on a broader deal definition and vintage). Both charts now sit side by side on the Financing Gap page. These are not in conflict: they measure venture activity on different bases. Each usage states its basis; they are never averaged.
Turnover velocity is on one WFE basis
The velocity chart (Canada TSX ~64%) is computed on a single basis — WFE 2024 annual value traded ÷ market cap — so every exchange is directly comparable. TSXV ~45% is a separate vault issuer-level figure (WFE does not split the junior board), labelled as such wherever it appears.
Korea KVIC figures are trade-press
The South Korea KVIC numbers are trade-press-sourced and should be read as indicative of scale, not audited.
Case studies are illustrative
The ~34 company studies illustrate mechanisms; they are not a statistical sample and don't support frequency claims.
CAD conversions are approximate
USD figures converted at ~US$1 = C$1.44. Cross-currency comparisons carry an FX margin.
The TMX sector cut is partial, not a forced rollup
Only 5 of the 9 core sectors map 1:1 onto a TMX sector; 2 more (Advanced Manufacturing, Infrastructure) are marked † as partial proxies only (Industrial Products & Services; Utilities & Pipelines); Defence and Agri-Food, plus the Auto & Batteries sub-sector, have no TMX sector and aren't shown. Trailing-24-month listing counts also mix true operating-company IPOs with TSXV graduations, RTOs, QTs and ETF/fund creations — only 6 TSX + 4 TSXV of the 58/126 all-in "new listings" are true IPOs.
WFE "listed companies" is an all-in count
The Global Exchanges explorer's listed-company totals (TMX 2025: 3,701, ≈ the MiG all-in 3,744) include ETFs, closed-end funds and other products wherever the exchange reports them — a different universe from the report's canonical 672 TSX operating issuers, which strips those out by design. Use the WFE figure for international listing-count comparisons; use 672 for the operating-issuer decline story.
Formation decline carries no international comparator
The BDC Index of New Entrepreneurial Activity (3.0 → 1.3 per 1,000, 2000–2022) has no defensible US / Israel counterpart on the chart: the only common cross-country metric, GEM's early-stage entrepreneurial-activity rate, measures a different construct and, for Canada, moves the opposite way. The omission is deliberate, not an oversight.

05 A note on standing
