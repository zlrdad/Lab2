02 Listed is not operating

The senior board's headline count is 71% funds.

Flip the TSX toggle above to Investment products and the picture inverts: of the TSX's 2,236 listings, only 672 are operating companies. The other 1,564 are ETFs, CDRs (Canadian Depositary Receipts — wrappers around foreign shares like Apple or Nvidia), closed-end funds and one dormant SPAC. When TMX reports "issuers up 8% year-on-year," that growth is almost entirely fund creation — not new productive-economy listings.

03 The mega-cap barbell

Half the senior board sits in 25 names.

Among those 672 operating TSX companies, market value is extraordinarily concentrated. The top 25 issuers hold over half the operating market cap; the top 100 hold 85%. The remaining hundreds share a thin tail — and the C$2–10B mid-cap tier, where a scaled Canadian champion would list, is almost empty. Concentration curve on the Feb-28-2026 TSX snapshot; the shape is stable across the May re-base.

The senior board's single largest tech name — Shopify, ~C$201B — exceeds the entire TSXV and CSE combined many times over. Scale lives in one place.
The pipeline, and its limits. Of the 222 TSX operating companies that graduated from the TSXV, 95 (43%) are mining — worth a combined C$608.7B at a median C$1.6B. Technology and Life Sciences produced only 19 and 21 graduates respectively, landing at a far smaller median scale (C$171M and C$141M). A further 260 TSXV companies now sit above the informal C$100M graduation threshold, but 212 of them are mining — only 10 technology, 4 life sciences. The venture board is a functioning escalator; it just runs mostly to one floor. 05 Interlisting, mapped

Nearly a third of the senior board already lists in the US.

A listed champion is never more than one step from a deeper market. 206 of the 672 operating TSX companies — 30.7% — are interlisted on a foreign exchange, and roughly 85% of those second listings are American. The share ticked up over just three months (from 199 of 668, 29.8%, in February): every net addition landed on a US venue. This is a scale / US-capital-access signal, not a flight — but the on-ramp to migration is already built. 06 The read

Three boards, one masking effect.

Read the headline listing counts and Canada's public markets look broad and growing. Strip the funds and read the operating companies — sector by sector, board by board — and the market is concentrated, mining-heavy on the juniors, and missing the mid-cap middle where champions belong. The lever is not cheaper listings; it is liquidity, coverage, and a domestic-demand base. The reconciliation to remember: 3,744 all-in listings, but only ~2,019 operating companies across the TSX and TSXV.
