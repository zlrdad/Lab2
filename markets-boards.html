02b Cross-listings

Where TSX issuers list abroad — and what it does to trading.

206 of the 672 TSX operating issuers (30.7%) are interlisted on a foreign exchange, 85% of them on a US venue (NYSE, NYSE American or Nasdaq) — the interlisting itself enabled by the Canada-US Multi-Jurisdictional Disclosure System (MJDS). This section maps the venue mix, the outsized turnover these names carry on the Canadian book, the eighteen-year interlisting trend and the cross-listed cut of named financings.

Read this loudly — TSX-side trading only
Every volume figure here is the Canadian-board side only. Value traded (vt) is the YTD value traded on the TSX / TSXV. The US-venue side of an interlisted name's trading is not in this dataset — and vault research finds much of it migrates off the Canadian book. Read the turnover shares below as "share of the Canadian tape," not total global trading. No cross-border offering split is available either.

Venue-history normalization verdict. The count uses the EXCL-OTC basis — an issuer counts as interlisted only with a genuine foreign exchange venue. The archive's pre-2018 vintages folded OTC (grey-market) quotations into the interlisted field, inflating 2011–17; the current workbook does not, and the EXCL-OTC series is continuous across the 2017→2018 vintage break (TSX 215→214) where the raw "any" series jumps. Venue-level history is left out — the group composition is only cleanly available for the current snapshot; across vintages the labels drift too much (NYSE MKT/Amex/Alternext, NASDAQ tiers, OMX/Nordic) to trust a per-group time series. The panel is a different source family (year-end workbooks) than the May-2026 Issuers List, so its 2025 endpoint (TSX 196) sits a little below the current-workbook 206.

As-of-date resolved — the honest join
The as-of-date basis (default) asks whether the issuer was interlisted at the December year-end of the financing year — computed from the archive panel, which carries per-year interlisting. That is the honest join and is preferred here. The Interlisted today basis instead flags deals by issuers still cross-listed in the May-2026 snapshot (fewer, because some have since delisted abroad or gone private). The full name-by-name searchable financings table — with a cross-listed only filter — lives on the History page, which owns the financings data.

Operating-issuer recipe
the filter that matters
Both boards strip Sector ∈ {ETP, CDR, Closed-End Funds, SPAC} — that is, exchange-traded products (ETP) and Canadian Depositary Receipts (CDR) alongside closed-end funds and SPACs. The TSXV additionally strips active CPC (Capital Pool Company) shells (161 rows). Result: TSX 2,236 → 672 operating; TSXV 1,508 → 1,347 operating. Cap bands are binned from the raw C$ cap (not the rounded C$M) so issuers never misband at the 10M / 150M edges.
Entity basis (WFE)
what each row consolidates
Each WFE entity is the whole operator: TMX = TSX + TSXV; Korea = KOSPI + KOSDAQ + KONEX; JPX = Prime + Standard + Growth; HKEX = Main + GEM. NSE India is excluded from Asia-Pacific market-cap totals (double-counts with BSE). WFE's listed-company count is all-in (TMX 2025 = 3,701), not the operating base.
Gap-fills
dashed / † / "no data"
LSE Group WFE series end 2020; 2021–25 filled from the LSE primary factsheets (Main + AIM, GBP→USD at BoE rates). Velocity / follow-on-share / avg-company-size for the fill years use the anchor's GBP-basis figures; listed_dom is filled 2021–23 only (2024–25 null); IPO count and avg IPO size are not filled. Korea & Nasdaq-US follow-on are zero-coded non-reports → null ("no data", never 0). A lone trailing-2025 follow-on zero after real history (ASX / Deutsche Börse / SIX) is also nulled.
CSE caveats
aggregates + value-traded
The CSE publishes no per-issuer market-cap file → aggregates only. Its concentration figures (top-10 46.2%, top-25 59.4%, top-50 71.3%) are value-traded shares, not market cap. The CSE headline counts 601 active issuers; its by-sector table sums to 600 (one unclassified / rounding) — recorded, not forced.

Issuer links
a convenience, not data
Company names link out to third-party profiles. Currently-listed issuers link to their Yahoo Finance quote page, built deterministically from the ticker plus its venue (TSX → .TO, TSXV → .V, CSE → .CN; class/unit tickers such as BAM.A become BAM-A). Delisted or unresolvable companies link to a quoted name search instead — never a ticker — because tickers get recycled and a dead symbol would resolve to a different company. The links are conveniences only; they add no data and no external scripts.
Refresh
how to rebuild
