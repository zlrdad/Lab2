02 What the roster teaches

Four patterns that decide the outcome.

Strip the individual stories back and the same four mechanics recur — each pointing at where an intervention would actually bind.

Exit is by acquisition
The credible champions leave chiefly by being bought (Tenstorrent, Element AI, Untether) or taken private (Magnet) — at the growth / public stage, by US buyers.
Foreign control = exit by another name
Fullscript and PointClickCare keep Canadian HQs but are US-PE-controlled. Control and upside leave before the jobs do — so intervene earlier.
Retention is achievable
StackAdapt, Wealthsimple, Neo, bootstrapped Geotab / Jane, repatriated MDA — domestic capital and control do anchor champions. They're just rare.
Control cuts both ways
Dual-class let Shopify stay independent — and let Magnet's controllers sell to US PE. Governance preserves the option; it doesn't compel staying.

03 A caveat worth naming

Canada's public-market wins are real — but they rest on two proofs, not an ecosystem.

The TSX has two genuine, durable technology successes, and they win in different ways. Shopify is the retention archetype — Canada's most valuable tech company (~C$202B), kept independent and TSX/NYSE-listed by founder control and a deliberate embedding in Canadian capital markets. Constellation Software is the permanent-capital compounder — a serial acquirer that has thrown off two more listed champions, Topicus and Lumine (both still controlled). But Topicus and Lumine are controlled spin-offs, not independent successes, so the compounding win concentrates in a single lineage (Constellation → Topicus → Lumine), with Descartes the one unrelated parallel. Two models — founder-controlled retention and permanent-capital compounding — each proven exactly once. Both are replicable; the ecosystem simply hasn't replicated them at any scale.

And the stakes are live. The TSX's thinnest tier is the C$2–10B mid-cap "missing middle" — and the companies that would fill it are exactly the private champions now deciding whether to stay, list abroad, or sell: Cohere, Waabi, Xanadu, Tenstorrent, the roster's at-risk cluster. AI sharpens the whole question: Canada trains ~10% of the world's top-tier AI researchers but captures under 2% of global AI venture capital, and almost none of its AI champions is on a path to a Canadian listing. Making "the second and third" possible is, at its core, an AI-champion-retention question — and it is being answered right now.

04 The read

The companies confirm the diagnosis.

Stays are possible but exceptional; the default trajectory is acquisition or a US listing, and foreign growth capital is the mechanism. Retention is achievable when domestic capital and control align — the comparators showed which mechanisms make it so. The report's final question is which of those levers Ontario should actually pull, and in what order.
