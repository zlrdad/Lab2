05 Concentration lab

How top-heavy is each market — really?

Canada's public market is concentrated, but not on a single name. The top-25 TSX operating issuers hold over half the market cap; the top 100 hold 85%. The ladder below is computed from the real caps at runtime and checked against the canonical figures (a console warning fires on any drift). The cross-exchange strip sets Canada's single-name concentration against the Asian markets now overtaking it — on deliberately mixed bases, so read it as a contrast, not a ranked table.

Operating-issuer recipe
the filter that matters
Both boards strip Sector ∈ {ETP, CDR, Closed-End Funds, SPAC} — that is, exchange-traded products (ETP) and Canadian Depositary Receipts (CDR) alongside closed-end funds and SPACs. The TSXV additionally strips active CPC (Capital Pool Company) shells (161 rows). Result: TSX 2,236 → 672 operating; TSXV 1,508 → 1,347 operating. Cap bands are binned from the raw C$ cap (not the rounded C$M) so issuers never misband at the 10M / 150M edges.
Entity basis (WFE)
what each row consolidates
Each WFE entity is the whole operator: TMX = TSX + TSXV; Korea = KOSPI + KOSDAQ + KONEX; JPX = Prime + Standard + Growth; HKEX = Main + GEM. NSE India is excluded from Asia-Pacific market-cap totals (double-counts with BSE). WFE's listed-company count is all-in (TMX 2025 = 3,701), not the operating base.
Gap-fills
dashed / † / "no data"
LSE Group WFE series end 2020; 2021–25 filled from the LSE primary factsheets (Main + AIM, GBP→USD at BoE rates). Velocity / follow-on-share / avg-company-size for the fill years use the anchor's GBP-basis figures; listed_dom is filled 2021–23 only (2024–25 null); IPO count and avg IPO size are not filled. Korea & Nasdaq-US follow-on are zero-coded non-reports → null ("no data", never 0). A lone trailing-2025 follow-on zero after real history (ASX / Deutsche Börse / SIX) is also nulled.
CSE caveats
aggregates + value-traded
The CSE publishes no per-issuer market-cap file → aggregates only. Its concentration figures (top-10 46.2%, top-25 59.4%, top-50 71.3%) are value-traded shares, not market cap. The CSE headline counts 601 active issuers; its by-sector table sums to 600 (one unclassified / rounding) — recorded, not forced.

Issuer links
a convenience, not data
Company names link out to third-party profiles. Currently-listed issuers link to their Yahoo Finance quote page, built deterministically from the ticker plus its venue (TSX → .TO, TSXV → .V, CSE → .CN; class/unit tickers such as BAM.A become BAM-A). Delisted or unresolvable companies link to a quoted name search instead — never a ticker — because tickers get recycled and a dead symbol would resolve to a different company. The links are conveniences only; they add no data and no external scripts.
Refresh
how to rebuild
Re-run python3 Data/refresh-scripts/markets_dashboard_data.py after dropping a newer TMX xlsx, WFE export, CSE analysis or LSE December factsheets. The pipeline asserts every reconciliation target before writing — on any failure it writes nothing and prints the discrepancy. Then re-run the HTML build step (build_markets_dashboard.py).
