02 Where Canada ranks

Big by size. Thin on conversion.

Ranked against all fourteen peers at once, Canada's position splits cleanly in two. On the size measures — its share of global equity, the scale of its economy — it sits near the top. On the conversion measures — R&D intensity, innovation rank — it sits near the bottom. Venture capital, unicorn production per head and tax competitiveness fall in between. The gap between the top block and the bottom block is the growth prize.

Equity-share rank is stamped on the end-2025 WFE-consistent basis — as are all bars on this page. By mid-2026 Canada's standing is basis-dependent. On the country-level basis (Bloomberg) it slips to about 5th in this set: Taiwan overtook Canada as the world's sixth-largest stock market on Apr 29, 2026, and Korea followed on May 7, 2026 — both AI- and semiconductor-driven. On the exchange-level basis (WFE, the vault's own monthly series) no crossover has yet occurred — TMX (US$5.00T, May 2026) still narrowly leads Korea (US$4.95T, +US$54bn) and sits well ahead of Taiwan (US$4.56T) — but the TMX–Korea gap has collapsed from ~US$1.3T in early 2024 to US$54bn. Both bases are true; they measure different universes, and on either the convergence is undeniable. An AI-weighted market closing fast on a commodities-and-financials one; it sharpens, rather than softens, the page's diagnosis.

Third by the sheer size of its stock market — and by mid-2026 in a photo finish with AI-heavy Taiwan and Korea (still ahead on the exchange-level count, passed on the country-level one). Fourteenth by how much it spends turning research into product. The distance between those ranks is what the report is about.

What's excluded, and why. Only rows with clean, unflagged cross-jurisdiction coverage are charted. The Compendium's four expansion comparators (Ireland, Switzerland, Norway, Estonia) are deliberately left out — their coverage carries ⚠️ flags and unreconciled bases (e.g. Ireland's GDP-vs-GNI* distortion, Estonia's cumulative-not-current unicorn counts). Taiwan's GII rank (~16th, "Chinese Taipei") is unconfirmed by WIPO, so it is shown as n/a and excluded from the innovation ranking. Capital-gains treatment is not charted here to avoid range-vs-effective apples-to-oranges — the innovation-specific CGT-relief lattice lives on What Works §04b.
04 The read

The numbers set up the evidence.

This page is the diagnosis in one matrix: a large, prosperous economy that under-invests in the conversion of research to companies and under-funds the venture stage that scales them. What Works, just read, supplied the mechanisms — how Israel, Sweden, Korea and the UK built what Canada has not; this explorer is the full instrument its four highlight charts pointed to. What remains is to watch the position play out in real companies — who stayed, who was bought, and who listed abroad.
