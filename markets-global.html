Full basis notes — partial point · entity basis · Taiwan & the constituent tier
2026 partial point (market cap only). The annual WFE export ends 2025; the 2026 point on the market-capitalisation indicator is spliced from the WFE monthly market-cap file — 2026 = latest month reported (per-exchange month varies; May/Jun 2026), covering 19 exchanges. It is drawn hollow + dashed + * as a partial/estimate, never a full year. This closes the chart-vs-rankings gap: Korea reads US$2.76T at 2025 year-end but US$4.95T by May 2026. The three ranking exchanges (TMX, Korea, Taiwan) are pinned to May 2026 — the latest month TMX reports — for consistency with the rankings strip below; every other covered exchange shows its own freshest month. Other indicators end at 2025.
Entity basis. Each WFE entity consolidates every board that operator runs: TMX = TSX + TSXV; Korea = KOSPI + KOSDAQ + KONEX; JPX = Prime + Standard + Growth; HKEX = Main + GEM. LSE Group WFE series end 2020; 2021–25 are gap-filled from the LSE primary factsheets (Main + AIM, GBP→USD at BoE rates) — dashed with a †; IPO count and average IPO size are not filled. Korea and Nasdaq-US follow-on are zero-coded non-reports in the WFE feed → shown as "no data", never 0. Derived indicators (turnover velocity, average company size, average IPO size, follow-on share, foreign-listing share) carry their formula in the source drawer.
Taiwan & the constituent tier (entity-basis hygiene). Taiwan is the one market WFE reports as two separate legal entities — the TWSE main board and TPEx (its growth board) — where peers arrive pre-consolidated. The picker now offers a derived Taiwan (combined) ◇ series: additive indicators (listings, market cap, value traded, capital raised, new-listing counts) are summed, and ratios (velocity, average size, follow-on & foreign share) are recomputed from the combined numerator/denominator — never averaged (combined 2024 velocity is ~143%, not the ~243% mean of the two boards). It is our computation, not a WFE-reported entity — drawn dashed with a ◇ marker; its 2026* point is a clean same-month (May) sum, US$3.23T (2025) → US$4.93T (May 2026). TPEx, the four individual Nasdaq Nordic/Baltic venues (use the Nasdaq Nordic & Baltics aggregate instead) and Euronext Oslo (Oslo Børs → Euronext, Jun 2019; market-cap series ends 2019) are moved to the collapsed Constituent & partial series group, each labelled with its coverage span. The rankings strip and concentration lab below use TWSE alone — the entity in the Bloomberg/WFE crossover story; a combined view would add ~US$0.37T (May 2026).

The ranking you've heard vs the one WFE measures.

"Taiwan and South Korea passed Canada in stock-market value" is a real 2026 headline — and a real WFE-basis non-event. The two numbers measure different universes. Below: the two bases side by side, the crossover timeline, and one developing footnote the news hasn't caught up with.

On the WFE exchange-level basis, TMX still leads both through the latest reported month — the gap to Korea has just collapsed to ~US$54bn from ~US$1.3T two years ago.
WFE counts each exchange separately and includes every listed company at its full global value — so a company listed in two places appears in both exchanges' totals (TMX's C$5.96T operating cap includes the entire global value of its 206 interlisted names). Bloomberg's country ranking instead builds one deduplicated universe per country of its domestically listed companies. Different universes, different answers — and both are internally consistent: on Bloomberg's ~US$4.4T country basis Taiwan (Apr 29) and Korea (May 7 2026) passed Canada, while on WFE's exchange basis (~US$5.0T) Canada still edges both through the latest reported month. See the PROV drawer for tiers, dates and the developing Korea reversal.

Korea's rally cracked hard in July 2026 — the KOSPI's steepest monthly drop on record (~23%, from a June 22 peak), which may already be undoing part of the Bloomberg-basis crossover. No tier 1–6 outlet has yet published a 'Canada retakes 7th' story, so this is flagged developing, not asserted as settled. One tier-8 real-time aggregator shows Korea below Canada; a KRW/FX back-of-envelope agrees; neither is Bloomberg-grade confirmation.

What the news actually cites. Every "Taiwan/Korea passed Canada" headline traces to one instrument — Bloomberg's own country-level compilation ("data compiled by Bloomberg"), a ~free-float / primary-listing basis over a narrower ~US$4.4T Canada universe, with no published methodology note. That is a different measurement from the WFE exchange-level market cap this dashboard reports (all TSX + TSXV listings). Both are real. Bloomberg rows are tier-6 (news media over a proprietary compilation); the WFE row is tier-1.
